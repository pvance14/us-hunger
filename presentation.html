The Pattern We Saw

Across every interview, a common thread emerged: volunteer coordination was manual, fragmented, and exhausting. Staff were spending their limited bandwidth on logistics — not community impact. We built for that gap.

The Solution

What Our Application Does

Four AI-powered systems that eliminate the highest-friction coordination tasks.

🤖
Core Feature

Autonomous Sub-Filling Agent

When a volunteer cancels, the system automatically works through the sub list — pinging one candidate at a time in 15-minute intervals until the shift is filled. No coordinator involvement needed.

📩
Core Feature

T-Minus Reminder Drip

Automated SMS sequences at T-72 hours (confirm/cancel), T-24 hours (logistics drop — bag color, map link), and T-2 hours (final nudge). Volunteers know exactly what to do without any manual texts.

📋
Core Feature

Compliance & Onboarding Portal

Volunteers self-onboard via mobile — uploading driver's license and insurance. The system tracks expiry dates and sends automated renewal nudges at 30, 7, and 0 days.

🗼
Core Feature

Control Tower Dashboard

A real-time coordinator view: live SMS pulse feed, staffing funnel, red-alert sidebar for danger-zone cancellations, and an impact ticker showing hours saved — built for glanceable decision-making.

Where We Go Next

Phase 1 stabilizes the logistics. Phase 2 expands the intelligence.

Natural Language AI

Conversational Scheduling

Replace rigid shortcodes (YES/NO/SUB) with an NLP engine that understands "I can come Thursday but not Friday" and responds in context.

Platform Sync

Bi-Directional Write-Back

Push confirmed volunteer statuses back to the CBO's source of truth — Google Sheets, ClickUp, Stockbox — automatically closing the loop.

Multi-Channel

Beyond SMS

WhatsApp integration for multilingual communities, automated voice calls (text-to-speech) for elderly volunteers on landlines, and email summaries.

Cross-Department

Unified Intake Module

One mobile form that triggers workflows across Legal, Food, and Hygiene departments — inspired directly by Elevate Utah's fragmented intake pain point.
